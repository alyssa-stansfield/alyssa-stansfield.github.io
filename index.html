About Me

Alyssa M. Stansfield, PhD

Alyssa is currently an NSF AGS Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Colorado State University working with Dr. Kristen Rasmussen. She graduated with her PhD in Atmospheric Science from Stony Brook University in May 2022 and with a BS in Meteorology from Rutgers University in May 2017. Her main postdoctoral project focuses on understanding how the 3D precipitation structures in tropical cyclones may change due to climate change.

Research

Precipitation Processes

Precipitation is essential for sustaining both ecosystems and society (agriculture, energy, recreation, manufacturing, etc...) but also can cause dangerous flooding and landslides; therefore understanding how precipitation is impacted by climate change is an important area of research. My research approaches precipitation in the context of the different storm sytems that produce it, such as tropical cyclones, mesoscale convective systems, atmospheric rivers, and extratropical storms. I use models across scales from global climate models to high-resolution model simulations of single storm events and observations from satellites, radars, and precipitation gauges to study storms and their precipitation.
